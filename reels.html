QUESTIONS

The honest FAQ.

Will this trigger YouTube's reused-content policy?

No. The reused-content policy targets channels that re-edit OTHER people's clips without significant transformation. When you compile your own original footage on your own channel, you're the rights-holder. YouTube's policy explicitly carves out "your own original creations." This is the entire reason the service exists in this form.

Can you use AI b-roll without getting flagged?

Yes, with discipline. YouTube's AI-disclosure label only kicks in for synthetic content that "shows real people doing things they didn't do" or "depicts realistic events that didn't happen." Atmospheric AI b-roll (cinematic landscapes, weather, abstract motion) layered over your actual voice doesn't qualify. We use it sparingly and intentionally, never as filler. The cinematic moves come from real footage and real edit decisions.

Who owns the work?

You. Always. We deliver the finished reel as your property under work-for-hire. Your channel, your monetization, your brand. We don't appear in credits unless you ask us to.

What's the turnaround?

Spec reel: 7–10 days from receiving your channel link and selected videos. Retainer reels: 7-day rough cut, 14-day finished delivery. One round of revisions on each. Rush available at +50%.

What if my channel doesn't have a deep back catalog yet?

If you have under 50 videos, the format probably doesn't work yet. Wait until you've shot 100+ pieces of content, then come back. We'll be honest about whether it's the right move. We've turned down inquiries before.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes after the 3-month minimum on The Flagship tier (the lowest retainer). The Channel and Director's Cut tiers are month-to-month from start. 30-day notice to cancel. Annual prepay discount: 10%.

What if my Spec reel doesn't hit 100K?

You owe nothing. You keep the reel. If it sat at, say, 60K, that's still meaningful for your channel — and we both learn the format isn't the right fit for your audience without spending a year on a retainer. Either way you're not out a dollar.

How do you decide who gets accepted for a Spec?

We're looking for: 100K+ subs, a deep enough back catalog (at least 100 videos), a niche where the audience responds emotionally to cinematic edits (motivation, heritage, business, spirituality, philosophy), and a creator who already has a back-end product so the win compounds beyond ad revenue. If you don't match those, we'll tell you. We'd rather decline than waste the cut.

Why is the Spec gated and pay-on-performance?

Two reasons. First, we don't have time to cut a Spec for everyone — only 2 a month. Second, asking you to pay before we prove the format works isn't fair to you. So we put the risk on us: we cut for free, and you only pay $1,500 if the reel crosses 100K views on your channel. That number isn't arbitrary — at 100K views in your niche, the reel has paid for itself in direct ad revenue alone, and you have a flagship asset that earns for years.

Who decides my Spec hit 100K views?

YouTube does. The view count on your published video is the single source of truth. We agree upfront on the upload window (your channel, your normal cadence), and we both watch the same number climb. If it crosses 100K, the invoice triggers. If it doesn't, no invoice ever sends.
